What is Food Sovereignty

Valarie Segrest describes food sovereignty as a way of healing or to get out of survival mode. One thing that she suggests is that if you're walking through the grocery store, bring your ancestors with you. Matika Wilbur comments that “Our spirit gets hungry.” Another aspect is to rewrite the narratives surrounding our current systems. For example, food deserts as a bad thing, while deserts have provided for indigenous people for thousands of years.

I-Collective

The I-Collective stands for four principles: Indigenous, Inspired, Innovative, and Independent. An autonomous group of Indigenous chefs, activists, herbalists, seed, and knowledge keepers, the I-Collective strives to open a dialogue and create a new narrative that highlights not only historical Indigenous contributions, but also promotes our community's resilience and innovations in gastronomy, agriculture, the arts, and society at large.
