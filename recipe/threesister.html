Algonquin Three Sisters Vegetables

The “Three Sisters” are three essential seeds to many Native American communities that consist of corn, beans, and squash. It is believed that when planted together, the Three Sisters work together to help one another to grow and survive. These crops all compliment one another nutritionally as well as in the garden. All three are able to be dried and used all year round, which was utilized by Native Americans. The tradition originated with the Haudenosaunee, also known as the Iroquois, who occupy the regions around the Great Lake in the Northeastern United States. Serve these tasty essential vegetables with steamed Wild Rice and a grilled Venison Steak!

List of ingredients

  • 2 C Baby Lima Beans (Fresh or Frozen)
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper
  • 1 Green Bell Pepper
  • 2 C Corn (Fresh or frozen whole kernels)
  • 2 C Eggplant
  • 2 C Fresh, Firm Roma Tomatoes
  • 2 C Yellow squash (1 medium)
  • 2 C Zucchini (1 medium)
  • 2 T Butter or Butter Substitute
  • 3 Cloves of Garlic
  • 1 c White Onion
  • 1/2 c Fresh Parsley
  • 1/4 t Pepper
  • 1/4 t Paprika
  • Morton Salt Substitute to taste

Steps:

  1. Chop all vegetables to bite size.
  2. Dice the parsley, onions and garlic.
  3. In medium cast-iron skillet heat oil, add onion, garlic and peppers and saute until soft.
  4. Steam squash, zucchini and lima beans until done and add to the skillet.
  5. Season with pepper, paprika.
  6. Season with Morton Salt Substitute to taste.
  7. Stir in the fresh parsley. Enjoy!
