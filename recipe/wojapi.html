Wojapi

Wojapi is a thick berry dish, sometimes the consistency of pudding.

List of ingredients

  • 4-5 cups Berries of your choice that are fresh and preferably growing wild (blueberries, grapes, chokecherries were used historically but can be bitter and must be pitted)
  • ½ cup water
  • Honey
  • Cornstarch

Steps:

  1. Clean the fruit
  2. Place in bowl and mash using potato masher (or a fork, but that takes longer)
  3. Add fruit and liquid to large saucepan and bring to boil—be careful not to scorch the fruit.
  4. Lower heat to a simmer and cook for about an hour, constantly checking.
  5. Taste and see if you need to add honey for sweetness—only add a little at a time.
  6. If you want the mixture to be thicker, either continue to simmer and “reduce” the mix (that is, the liquid evaporates rendering the taste more intense and the mix thicker) or add a small amount of cornstarch. If you go with the latter option, place 1 T of cornstarch into small bowl and add cold water to that.
  7. Stir until you have a desired consistency (add more water or starch if needed).
  8. Then slowly add to hot pot of berries and stir.
