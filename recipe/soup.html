Potato and Wild Rice Soup

Poyha is a venison dish that originated from the Cherokee tribe. You can almost think of it as an old Native American recipe for meatloaf. This hearty meal originally called for wild onions & buffalo berries but is modified to fit modern ingredients.

List of ingredients

  • 6 strips bacon
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 C. washed and fully cooked Red Lake Nation Wild Rice
  • 3 C. Chicken Stock
  • 2 cans (10.75 oz.) Cream of Potato soup
  • 2 C. half & half
  • Â½ C. sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 2 C. shredded cheddar cheese

Steps:

  1. Combine all ingredients (except cheese & half & half) in 3 quart saucepan or Dutch oven.
  2. Simmer about 20-30 minutes.
  3. Turn off heat and gently pour in half in half.
