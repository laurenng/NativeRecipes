Acorn Squash and Wild Rice

Wild rice is a staple to Ojibwe tribes who came from the Midwestern United States. Wild rice is easy to prepare and available all year round. The month of september was called “ricing moon” where the tribes set up camp next to the lakes in preparation for the harvest. They spent weeks harvesting and processing the wild rice and then celebrated with a festival where they cooked and ate the first finished rice of the “ricing moon”.

List of ingredients

  • 7 cup water 2 cups wild rice
  • 3 small acorn squash cut in half & seeded 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cups onions finely chopped
  • 2 tsp dried sage leaves crumbled
  • 2 tbl lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup toasted pecans chopped
  • 1/4 cup parsley chopped

Steps:

  1. Bring water and rice to boil in large heavy saucepan
  2. Reduce heat; cover and simmer until rice is tender about 1 hour
  3. Drain
  4. Transfer rice to large bowl
  5. Place squash cut side down on greased baking sheet
  6. Bake in preheated 375 F (190 C) oven until tender about 40 minutes
  7. Cool
  8. Using spoon scoop out pulp from squash leaving 1/4 inch (.5 cm) shell; reserve shells
  9. Transfer pulp to medium bowl
  10. Reduce oven to 350 F (180 C)
  11. Melt butter in large non-stick skillet over medium heat
  12. Add onions; saute until very tender about 15 minutes
  13. Add sage; stir 2 minutes
  14. Add rice squash pulp and lemon juice; stir until mixed breaking up squash pulp into smaller pieces
  15. Mix in cranberries Pecans and parsley
  16. Season with salt and pepper
  17. Divide rice mixture among reserved squash shells
  18. Place in roasting pan
  19. Bake squash until filling is heated through about 25 minutes.
