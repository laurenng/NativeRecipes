Seminole Sauteed Alligator Tail

Alligator hunting was a food source for Southeastern Native Americans for thousands of years. Alligators became a part of their livelihood as they showed them off as roadside attractions to make money. Alligator wrestling became popular as Seminole and Miccosukee tribes learned how to “handle” the animals. In the Everglades, surviving off what Mother Nature provided meant surviving off alligators. The tail, being the most meaty part, was used for food while the skin was used to trade for things they could not grow.

List of ingredients

  • 2 lbs Alligator Tail Meat
  • 2 Eggs, beaten
  • 1 C Flour
  • 1/2 t Garlic Powder
  • 1 t Paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 C Butter to saute

Steps:

  1. Clean and cut the alligator tail meat into strips lengthwise, 4x2-inch wide.
  2. Sprinkle with lemon juice.
  3. Mix flour, garlic powder, paprika, salt and pepper.
  4. Blot the alligator pieces, and dip into flour, then into beaten eggs.
  5. Let eggs drip off and dip into flour again.
  6. Melt the butter in a large skillet and saute the alligator pieces quickly. Do not overcook or they will be tough.
  7. As soon as the meat is browned add 2 cups of water and 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice to the skillet.
  8. Cover and simmer for about 2 hours or until very tender.
  9. Serve immediately.
